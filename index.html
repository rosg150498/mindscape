About Us

At Mindscape, we believe that mental health is just as important as physical health, and we strive to create a safe and welcoming space where individuals can find the resources and support they need to take care of their mental well-being.

Our team is made up of mental health professionals, advocates, and individuals with lived experience who are passionate about breaking down the stigma surrounding mental health and providing accessible and evidence-based information and resources.

We understand that seeking help for mental health concerns can be difficult, and we are here to help guide you on your journey towards healing and recovery.

Symptoms

Behavioural

  • Compulsive or Repetitive Behaviours
  • Changes in sleeping patterns
  • Decreased or Increased Appetite
  • Restlessness / Agitation
  • Social Isolation or Withdrawn
  • Self Harm or Suicidal Behaviour
  • Increase use of Drugs and Alcohol
  • Aggressive or Violent Behaviour

Cognitive

  • Difficulty concentrating or making decisions
  • Delusions or Hallucinations
  • Inability to think clearly or logically to make judgements
  • Memory problems or Forgetfulness
  • Intrusive or Obsessive thoughts
  • Racing thoughts or a sense of overwhelm

Emotional

  • Anxiety or Excessive worrying
  • Feelings of Numbness or Emptiness
  • Loss of Interest in Enjoyed Activties
  • Mood Swings or Emotional Instability
  • Anger or Irritability

Physical

  • Aches and Pains, such as Back Pain or Muscle Tension
  • Changes in Appetite or Weight
  • Fatigue or Lack of Energy
  • Headaches or Migraines
  • Digestive Problems, such as Nausea or Diarrhea
  • Palpitations or Rapid Heartbeat
  • Chills or Sweating

Social

  • Avoidance of Activities or Social Situations
  • Difficulty Communicating or Expressing Oneself
  • Difficulty Maintaining Employment or School Attendance
  • Strained Relationships with Family or Friends
  • Lack of Interest in Personal Hygiene or Grooming
  • Poor Judgment or Decision Making skills
  • Financial problems
  • Legal Problems

Seeking Help and Treatment

If you or someone you know is struggling with mental health, it is important to seek help and treatment as soon as possible. There are a variety of options available, including therapy, medication, support groups, and more. It's okay to ask for help, and there's no shame in seeking treatment. This section of our website provides information on some of the resources available to you, including hotlines, mental health clinics, and therapists. Remember, taking care of your mental health is just as important as taking care of your physical health.

Therapy and Counseling

  • Cognitive-behavioral therapy (CBT): A type of talk therapy that helps individuals identify and change negative patterns of thinking and behavior
  • Behavioural activation: This form of talk therapy focuses on guiding individuals with depression to take achievable, concrete actions towards regaining enjoyment in their life.
  • Interpersonal therapy (IPT): A type of psychotherapy that focuses on helping individuals with depression recognize and resolve issues within their relationships with family, friends, and romantic partners.
  • Mindfulness-based cognitive therapy (MBCT) is a type of therapy that combines mindfulness meditation practices with cognitive-behavioral therapy techniques. This therapy helps individuals to become more aware of their thoughts and emotions as they occur in the present moment.

Medication

  • Antidepressants: Medications primarily prescribed for treating clinical depression. However, they can also be helpful in managing other conditions such as obsessive-compulsive disorder (OCD), generalized anxiety disorder, and post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD). Additionally, antidepressants may be used as a treatment option for chronic pain management.
  • Anti-anxiety medications are prescribed to help alleviate symptoms of anxiety, and may also be used to aid with sleep if the individual is experiencing severe insomnia.
  • Selective serotonin reuptake inhibitors (SSRIs) : A common type of antidepressant medication that are primarily prescribed to manage depression, especially when it is persistent or severe. They are often used in conjunction with a type of talk therapy called cognitive-behavioral therapy (CBT). SSRIs are also effective in treating other mental health conditions such as generalized anxiety disorder (GAD), obsessive-compulsive disorder (OCD), panic disorder, severe phobias like social phobia, bulimia, and post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD).
  • Mood stabilizers: Medications that can help regulate and stabilize mood, preventing extreme mood swings. These medications are often prescribed for individuals with a bipolar disorder.

Support Group

  • To find support groups for challenges that may arise, individuals can utilize various resources, including online searches, healthcare providers, nonprofit organizations, community centers, places of worship, and word of mouth recommendations. Participating in peer support programmes can be an additional beneficial option for individuals experiencing challenges such as:

  • Addiction
  • Anxiety
  • Bereavement
  • Dementia
  • Divorce
  • Depression
  • Other Mental Health Conditions
  • Relationship problems

Self Care

  • Engaging in self-care practices can help individiuals take care of their physical, mental, and emotional well-being which helps to reduce stress, improve mood, and promote overall wellness. While self-care looks different for everyone, there are several common practices that individuals can try to incorporate into their daily routines. Here are some examples of self-care practices that may be helpful in promoting mental health:

  • Exercise: Engaging in physical activity such as jogging, yoga, or weightlifting to reduce stress and improve mood
  • Headspace: A website and app that provides guided meditation and mindfulness exercises
  • Journaling: Writing down thoughts and feelings as a way to process and cope with stress and difficult emotions
  • Meditation: A practice of focusing the mind and achieving a state of calm and relaxation

Resources and Support

Living with mental illness can be difficult, but it's important to know that you're not alone. There are many resources and support systems available to help you through your journey. We offer access to valuable information on various resources and support systems to assist you on your journey of living with mental illness such as groups, self-help resources, and more. Whether you're looking for a listening ear or simply want to connect with others who understand what you're going through, there are many options available to you. Remember, seeking support is a sign of strength, not weakness. You deserve to have a happy and healthy life, and we're here to help you achieve and restore that.

Crisis Hotlines

  • National Suicide Prevention Helpline UK - 0800 689 5652: This helpline provides a compassionate listening service for individuals experiencing thoughts of suicide. It operates from 6pm to midnight daily. If you are unable to reach the main helpline number mentioned earlier, you can call 0800 689 0880 as an alternative.
  • HOPELINEUK - 0800 068 41 41 or text 07860039967: This helpline offers confidential support for individuals under the age of 35 who are at risk of suicide, as well as those who are concerned about them. It is available every day from 9am to midnight.
  • Sane - 0300 304 7000: Provides a helpline and emotional support to individuals impacted by mental health problems, offering valuable information and guidance.

Online Resources

  • Online Therapy Platforms: Platforms such as Amwell, BetterHelp and Talkspace offer online therapy services, connecting individuals with licensed therapists through video calls, messaging, or phone sessions. These platforms provide accessible and convenient mental health support.It is important to recognise that the quality of therapy can vary among therapists on these platforms, and they may not be the most suitable options for individuals with severe mental health conditions that require more intensive or in-person care.
  • Amwell: Amwell is a telemedicine platform offering various healthcare services, including mental health consultations with psychiatrists or therapists. It provides convenient access to professionals through video calls or phone consultations. While Amwell can be a convenient option for initial evaluations, medication management, or follow-up appointments, it may not offer the same level of ongoing therapeutic support found in dedicated online therapy platforms like BetterHelp or Talkspace.
  • BetterHelp: BetterHelp is an online counseling platform that enables individuals to connect with licensed therapists via text, chat, phone, or video sessions. It offers a convenient and accessible way to receive therapy from the comfort of your home. The platform aims to match individuals with therapists who suit their specific needs and preferences.
  • Talkspace: Talkspace is an online therapy platform that grants individuals access to licensed therapists through text, voice, or video messaging. It provides flexible therapy options, making it suitable for individuals who prefer written communication or have scheduling constraints.
